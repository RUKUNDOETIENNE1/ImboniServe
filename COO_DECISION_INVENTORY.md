# COO Decision Inventory — Hospitality Operations

**Document Version**: 1.0  
**Phase**: 1.2E-A COO Intelligence Architecture & Reality Design Review  
**Date**: June 24, 2026  
**Role**: Chief Operating Officer & Hospitality Operations Director  

---

## Executive Summary

**Mission**: Identify actual decisions that consume COO attention in hospitality operations

**Scope**: Hotels, Restaurants, Hospitality Groups

**Finding**: COO decisions are fundamentally different from CEO/CFO decisions

**Key Insight**: COOs manage **execution quality**, not strategy or finance

---

## Decision Framework

### COO vs CEO vs CFO

**CEO Decisions**: What should we do? (Strategy)
- Market expansion
- Product development
- Competitive positioning

**CFO Decisions**: Can we afford it? (Finance)
- Revenue optimization
- Cost management
- Financial risk

**COO Decisions**: Are we executing well? (Operations)
- Service delivery quality
- Operational consistency
- Execution bottlenecks
- Staff effectiveness

---

## Daily Decisions (6am - 11pm)

### Morning Operations (6am - 10am)

#### Decision 1: Shift Coverage Crisis Management
**Frequency**: 2-3x per week

**Trigger**:
- Staff call-out
- No-show
- Last-minute sick leave

**Decision Required**:
- Pull from which location?
- Approve overtime?
- Reduce service scope?
- Call emergency backup?

**Time Pressure**: 15-30 minutes

**Impact**: Immediate service quality

**Current Pain**: COO learns about this via phone call, not system

---

#### Decision 2: Service Quality Incident Response
**Frequency**: Daily (multi-location operations)

**Trigger**:
- Customer complaint escalation
- Health inspection issue
- Safety incident
- Service failure

**Decision Required**:
- Immediate intervention needed?
- Which manager to deploy?
- Escalate to CEO/legal?
- Document for pattern analysis?

**Time Pressure**: Immediate (5-15 minutes)

**Impact**: Brand reputation, legal risk

**Current Pain**: COO learns via email/phone, no pattern visibility

---

#### Decision 3: Supply Chain Disruption Response
**Frequency**: 1-2x per week

**Trigger**:
- Vendor delivery failure
- Inventory shortage
- Quality issue with supplies

**Decision Required**:

- Emergency procurement?

**Time Pressure**: 1-2 hours

**Impact**: Service continuity

**Current Pain**: No visibility into inventory levels across locations

## COO vs CEO/CFO Decision Comparison

### CEO Decisions
- **Horizon**: Months to years
- **Focus**: Strategy, growth, market
- **Data**: Market trends, competitive intelligence
- **Frequency**: Weekly to monthly

### CFO Decisions
- **Horizon**: Weeks to quarters
- **Focus**: Revenue, costs, financial health
- **Data**: Financial metrics, KPIs
- **Frequency**: Daily to weekly

### COO Decisions
- **Horizon**: Minutes to weeks
- **Focus**: Execution quality, operational consistency
- **Data**: Operational signals, service metrics
- **Frequency**: Multiple times daily to monthly

**Key Difference**: COO decisions are **execution-focused** and **time-sensitive**
