**Issue**: 7.2% MRR decline triggers CRITICAL (should be WARNING)

**Impact on Trust**:
- CFO escalates to board
- Board questions why 7.2% is "CRITICAL"
- CFO checks KPI Catalog: "WARNING for 5-10%"
- **Trust damaged**: System contradicts governance

**Frequency**: HIGH (every 5-10% MRR decline)

**Trust Damage**: SEVERE

**Fix**: Align severity with KPI Catalog

**Effort**: 5 minutes

**Must Fix**: ✅ YES

## Trust Erosion Scenarios

### Scenario 1: False CRITICAL Alert

**Situation**: MRR declines 6.5%

**System**: CRITICAL alert

**CFO Action**: Escalates to board, prepares emergency presentation

**Board Response**: "Why is 6.5% decline a crisis? KPI Catalog says WARNING for 5-10%"

**CFO Reaction**: Embarrassed, questions system accuracy

**Trust Impact**: -20 points

**Frequency**: HIGH

**Cumulative Impact**: Trust erodes quickly

---

### Scenario 2: Missed Deterioration

**Situation**: Subscriptions declining 3.5% for 3 months

**System**: No alert (below threshold)

**CFO**: Doesn't notice until month 4 when decline crosses 5%

**CFO Reaction**: "Why didn't you tell me sooner?"

**Trust Impact**: -15 points

**Frequency**: MEDIUM

**Cumulative Impact**: System perceived as incomplete

---

### Scenario 3: Can't Prioritize

**Situation**: Payment failures + Reconciliation backlog both WARNING

**System**: Both shown, no financial impact

**CFO**: "Which one should I fix first?"

**System**: [no answer]

**CFO**: Manually calculates revenue impact, fixes payment first

**CFO Reaction**: "Why do I have to do this myself?"

**Trust Impact**: -10 points

**Frequency**: MEDIUM

**Cumulative Impact**: System perceived as not helpful

---

### Scenario 4: Generic Advice Doesn't Work

**Situation**: Churn at 8.5%

**System**: "Strengthen customer success programs"

**CFO**: Allocates resources to customer success

**Result**: Churn doesn't improve (root cause was product issues, not customer success)

**CFO Reaction**: "The system gave me wrong advice"

**Trust Impact**: -25 points

**Frequency**: LOW

**Cumulative Impact**: One bad experience can destroy trust
